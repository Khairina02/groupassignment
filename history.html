HISTORY OF KAMPUNG BARU


Who established Kampung Baru?
The British colonial government established Kampung Bharu, as a planned Malay village in the late 19th century.

Who gave the land?
Sultan Alauddin Sulaiman Syah Raja Muda Musa (Sultan Sulaiman) of Selangor gave the Malays 222 acres of land in Kuala Lumpur in 1899.

Why?
Its goal was to maintain Malay culture and traditions in the midst of the brisk urbanisation brought on by the profitable tin industry.

The traditional wooden houses in Kampong Bharu have maintained their distinctive architectural style over the years, decorated with intricate carvings and Malay motifs to symbolise Malaysia's rich heritage.

The neighbourhood continues to be a representation of cultural preservation and a harmonious relationship between tradition and modernity despite challenges posed by urbanisation.
